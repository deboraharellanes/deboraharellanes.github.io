Point Adjustment

  1. Let the car set over night so it will be cold
  2. Remove the distributer cap and the rotor
  3. Turn the engine by hand until the high point of the cam lobe is even with the fiber block on the movable breaker point
  4. Use a screwdriver to loosen the locking screw of the stationary breaker point
  5. Manipulate the stationary point plate so that the clearance between the points is .016"
  6. Tighten the locking screw of the stationary point
  7. Recheck the gap, because it might have changed since tightening the locking screw

Setting the Timing

  1. Turn the engine by hand until the appropriate mark on the crankshaft pulley is lined up with the crank case dividing line
  2. Loosen the clamp screw at the base of the distributor
  3. Attach the lead of a test lamp to terminal 1 of the ignition coil and ground the test lamp
  4. Turn the ignition switch on and rotate the distributor body clockwise until the contact points close and then rotate it slowly counter-clockwise until the points begin to open and the lamp lights
  5. Without moving the distributor, tighten the clamp screw at the base of the distributor
  6. Recheck the adjustment by turning the crankshaft pulley a half turn counter-clockwise, and then turn clockwise until the mark is 1 inch within the dividing line
  7. Tap the right side of the fan belt to check if your adjustment is accurate. Clockwise rotations stop the timing. Counter-clockwise rotate the timing
