Four Stroke Cycle

Three components:
  1. Gas-air mixture
  2. Spark
  3. Compression (the rings and valves)
Four strokes:
  1. Intake
  2. Compression
  3. Power
  4. Exhaust

Intake Stroke: Intake valve opens. Gas-air mixture enters the combustion chamber.

Compression Stroke: Intake and exhaust valves close. Piston thrusts into the high pressured gas-air mixture filled compression chamber.

Power Stroke: Spark ignites in the compression chamber. Explosion pushes the piston back.

Exhaust Stroke: Exhaust valves open. Piston thrusts back into the combustion chamber and pushes the spent gas-air mixture out through the tailpipe.


Engine parts are all synchronized to actuate combustion momentum-driving the four strokes. One engine revolution
completes the four stroke cycle. The rotation of the crankshaft pulley is timed to activate each piston in its cylinder.

  1. The first 90° of the turn activates the first piston in its cylinder #
  2. At 180° cylinder #2 piston is activated
  3. At 270° cylinder #3 piston is activated
  4. At360 ° cylinder #4 piston is activated

Each piston/cylinder action is timed in milliseconds, and propels the next stroke, continuously repeating the cycle, approximately 800 to 900 RPMs.
The rotor (inside the distributer), rotates-triggerering signals sent through the spark plug wires. This interconnects with the distributor drive shaft
which interconnects with the camshaft and crankshaft and drives the pistons inside the cylinders that form the compression chamber.
