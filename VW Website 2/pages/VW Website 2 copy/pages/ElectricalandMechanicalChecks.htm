Battery

In a VW Beetle the battery is stored under the back seat. Remove the back seat and check for battery terminal corrosion. Replace any corroded cables. A battery can last up to 5 years if the electrolite level is always covering the electrode plates. To check the water / electrolyte levels, take the caps off of the battery. If the electrodes are not covered, pour distilled water into the battery.

Check the electrical components and replace any frayed or cracked wire connections:

  • coil
  • generator
  • voltage regulator
  • starter

Check the mechanical components:

  • Fuel-line
  • Vacuum-line connections
  • Tighten the carburetor attaching nuts
  • Choke and carburetor linkages
  • Air cleaner and fuel filter
  • Fan belt deflection

Starter motor, Solenoid, & Ignition Switch Operation:

The solenoid has 3 connections that all must be checked for tightness

When you start your car, a 12 volt current from the ignition switch activates the solenoid through a connection on the top of the solenoid. This engages the pinion gear into the flywheel teeth, which turns the flywheel, and engages the starter motor. At the same time, the two connections on the other end of the solenoid, one to the battery (for a power supply) and another one directly to the starter motor has the opposite end of the pinion gear connected to it, which completes the circuit. This engages the starter motor, which turns the gear, which turns the flywheel, which starts the engine.
