Idling Speed and Slow-speed Mixture Screws

Turning the mixture screw clockwise will lean the mixture and turning it clockwise will enrich it.

  1. Turn on the engine. Adjust the slow-speed mixture control screw to an idling speed of 850-950 RPM (Revolutions per Minute)
  2. With the engine running at the proper idle speed, turn the slow-speed mixture control screw slowly clockwise until the engine speed begins to slow down. Then slowly turn it counter-clockwise until the engine is running smoothly again
  3. Turn the mixture control screw another 1/4 counter-clockwise rotation
  4. Re-adjust the idling speed if neccessary

Quickly depress and release the accelerator and then leave the engine to run. If the engine stalls, the adjustment is incorrect. Too much gasoline and not enough air makes the mixture to rich. Excess fuel consumption will cause the engine to stall when you release the accelerator. Too much air and not enough gasoline makes the mixture lean. Excess air will burn or warp the exhaust valves.
