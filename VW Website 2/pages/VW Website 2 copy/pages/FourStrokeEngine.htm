The Four Stroke Cycle

Three components actuate the four stroke process:
  1. Gas-air mixture
  2. Spark
  3. Compression (the rings and valves)
The four strokes are:
  1. Intake
  2. Compression
  3. Power
  4. Exhaust

In the Intake Stroke the intake valve opens and the gas-air mixture enters the combustion chamber.

In the Compression Stroke the intake and exhaust valves close. With maximum force, the piston thrusts into the high pressured gas-air mixture filled compression chamber.

In the Power Stroke a spark ignites in the compression chamber and the explosion pushes the piston back.

In the Exhaust Stroke the exhaust valves open. The piston thrusts back into the combustion chamber and pushes the spent gas-air mixture out through the tailpipe.

All the engine parts are synchronized to actuate the combustion momentum that drives the four strokes. One revolution of the engine makes the complete four stroke cycle. Each rotation of the crankshaft pulley is timed to activate one piston in its cylinder. The first 90° angle turn activates the piston in cylinder # 2. The 180° angle turn activates the piston in cylinder # 3. The 270° angle turn activates the piston in cylinder # 4. The 360 °or 0° angle turn activates the piston in cylinder # 1. Each piston/cylinder action propels the next stroke, in a continuous, repeating cycle, one after the other, timed in milliseconds, at a rate of approximately 800 to 900 RPMs (revolutions per minute). When the rotor inside the distributer, rotates, it triggers signals through the spark plug wires. This interconnects with the distributor drive shaft which interconnects with the camshaft and crankshaft which drive the pistons inside the cylinders that form the compression chamber.
