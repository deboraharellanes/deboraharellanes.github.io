Spark Plug Electrodes

Clean and gap the spark plugs at 6,000 miles and replace them at 12,000 miles. Gap between .024" and .028". Spark plugs have 14mm thread and a heat range of 145°.

If the spark plugs are spaced with a wide gap, the engine will be harder to start, and will miss at high speeds. If the spark plugs are spaced with a narrow gap, the engine won't miss at high speeds and will start easier, but it won't idle well. Gap the spark plugs to .020" to improve the engines ability to start in cold temperatures.

The condition of the electrodes and the insulation on the spark plugs determine the stoichiometric ratio and can give an idea of how the engine is running:

Electrode Trouble Shooting

Medium gray or brown
  • Stoichiometric ratio is correct
Black
  • Stoichiometric ratio is too rich
Light gray or white
  • Stoichiometric ratio is too lean
Spark plugs are oiled up
  • Spark plugs or the piston rings are failing
