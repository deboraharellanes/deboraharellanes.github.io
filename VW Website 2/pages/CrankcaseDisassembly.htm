Crankshaft and Camshaft

After you remove the cylinder and pistons and the outer engine parts, you can then spilt apart the engine. The crankcase has two sides or two halves that fit together to house the crankshaft and the camshaft. The crankshaft is mounted inside the upper half of the crankcase. The front end of the crankshaft attaches to the crankshaft pulley on the front side of the crankcase. The back end of the crankshaft attaches to the flywheel on the backside of the crankcase, which continues on to the clutch plate and then the transmission. The flywheel is a large circular gear. The flywheel's diameter fills up most of the space on the back end of the crankcase.

The camshaft is mounted inside the lower half of the crankcase. The crankshaft and the camshaft each have a gear on the front side that run together and propels the engine. The front end of the camshaft attaches to the oil pump on the front side of the crankcase. Inside the crankcase, the camshaft attaches with a gear that leads to the oil tube and then to the oil sump and the oil screen. The back end of the camshaft does not attach to any moving parts. It falls into the diameter space of the flywheel, of which the crankshaft is attached. The camshaft must be completely submerged in oil. When the oil level is getting low, the oil light will come on. Enough oil must be added to resubmerge the camshaft. If no oil is added, eventually your engine will seize up, stop running, and damage the engine. The engine will need to be rebuilt.

The two halves of the crankcase are factory made, finely machined to fit together very precisely and specifically. You can't mix and match halves from different crankcases. You can only use these two halves of the crankcase together that were factory made to go with each other.

Opening the Crankcase

  1. Remove the oil strainer, oil pressure switch, and crank case nuts
  2. In the right half of the crankcase, use the retaining springs to hold the cam followers in place
  3. Break the seal between the crankcase halves with a rubber hammer. Do not use sharp tools to break the seal of the two halves because it will cause serious damage
  4. After the seal between the two halves is broken, remove the right half of the crankcase, the crankshaft oil seal and the camshaft end plug
  5. Lift out the camshaft and crankshaft
  6. Remove the cam followers, bearing shells, and the oil pressure relief valve
